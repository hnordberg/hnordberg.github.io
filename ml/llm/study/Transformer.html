The Bottleneck of Recurrence

Prior to the Transformer, the dominant architectures for sequence transduction tasks (like language translation) were recurrent neural networks (such as LSTM), mapping sequences to a fixed hidden state \( h_t \) as a function of the previous state \( h_{t-1} \) and the input at position \( t \).

The Sequential Bottleneck

Common misconception: RNNs were replaced solely because of vanishing gradients. While true that standard RNNs struggle to learn long-range dependencies, the fatal flaw in the era of GPU hardware is that their sequential nature fundamentally precludes parallelization across the sequence. You cannot compute \( h_t \) until you have fully evaluated \( h_{t-1} \). The Transformer discards recurrence entirely, enabling full parallel computation across sequence tokens.
