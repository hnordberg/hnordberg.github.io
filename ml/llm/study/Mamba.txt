13:T69b,<p>By late 2023 the <a href="/ml/llm/study/Transformer">Transformer</a> had dominated sequence modeling for six years, but its quadratic cost in sequence length \( L \) kept forcing context windows to grow through systems work rather than architectural progress. A parallel line of research on <a href="/ml/wiki/topics/state-space-models-mamba">structured state space models</a> (S4, H3, <a href="/ml/wiki/topics/hyena-long-convolutions">Hyena</a>) offered \( O(L) \) training and \( O(1) \) per-step inference by replacing <a href="/ml/wiki/topics/self-attention">self-attention</a> with a linear recurrence over a learned continuous-time dynamical system. On long-range benchmarks like Path-X these models were state of the art; on language modeling they lagged Transformers of the same parameter count by a visible margin.</p><p>Mamba identifies the cause of that gap and fixes it. Prior SSMs are <b>linear time-invariant</b> (LTI): the same recurrence matrices apply at every step, so the model cannot decide to attend to a specific token or ignore a filler one. The paper introduces a <b>selection mechanism</b> that makes the step size \( \Delta \) and the input/output projections \( B, C \) functions of the current token, breaking time-invariance and letting the hidden state copy, skip, or erase content as needed. A hardware-aware parallel scan keeps the recurrence \( O(L) \) in wall-clock time despite the loss of convolutional structure.
