Next-Token Prediction Objective (Causal Language Modeling)

<|

The core foundational causal language modeling training architecture driving autoregressive text generators optimizing exactly evaluating negative log-likelihood bounds determining accurate prediction representations maximizing sequentially.

Formulation. Given a sequence of tokens \( w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_T \), the joint probabilistic sequence is factorized via the chain rule of purely directional probability metrics uniformly:

\[ P(w_1, \ldots, w_T) = \prod_{t=1}^{T} P_\theta(w_t \mid w_1, \ldots, w_{t-1}) \]

The training loss converges targeting strictly the overall average negative log-likelihood metrics calculating precisely parallelized positions:

\[ \mathcal{L}(\theta) = -\frac{1}{T} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \log P_\theta(w_t \mid w_{1:t-1}) \]
