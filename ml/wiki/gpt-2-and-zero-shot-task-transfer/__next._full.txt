  </svg><p>GPT-2 extended GPT-1's decoder-only Transformer from 117M to 1.5B parameters trained on WebText, a 40 GB internet text corpus. The conceptual breakthrough was reframing task-specific inference as an implicit sub-task of standard language modeling.</p>

  <p>In standard supervised learning, a model predicts labels from input: \(P(\text{output} \mid \text{input})\). GPT-2 models the joint distribution autoregressively and conditions on a task description via prompt structure:</p>

  \[P(\text{output} \mid \text{input}, \text{task description})\]

  <p>Since the training corpus is large and extremely diverse, the standard language modeling objective already implicitly covers many translation and parsing tasks just to predict internet text sequences. The objective is simply Next-Token Prediction over sequences:</p>

  \[L = -\sum_{i} \log P(u_i \mid u_{i-k}, \ldots, u_{i-1}; \Theta)\]

  <p>When the model receives exactly the pattern "Translate English to French: [text] =&gt;", the context biases the next-token attention matrices towards emitting French words because such structures occurred frequently on WebText.</p>

  <p>On benchmark tasks like reading comprehension, translation, and text completion, GPT-2 established state-of-the-art results purely in the zero-shot setting against fully supervised models.
