  </svg><p>Standard dense neural networks activate every parameter for every input. Mixture of Experts (MoE) breaks this constraint: the model contains \(n\) expert networks \(E_1, \ldots, E_n\) (typically block feed-forward layers), and a gating network \(G\) selects which experts process each individual input token. For an input \(x\), the output is:</p>

  \[y = \sum_{i=1}^{n} G(x)_i \cdot E_i(x)\]

  <p>where \(G(x) \in \mathbb{R}^n\) is sparse — most entries are forced to zero structurally. Sparsity is achieved by keeping only the top-\(k\) gate values (typically \(k = 1\) or \(2\)) and zeroing the rest unconditionally via a <b>noisy top-k gating</b> mechanism:</p>

  \[G(x) = \text{Softmax}\big(\text{TopK}(H(x), k)\big), \quad H(x)_i = (x \cdot W_g)_i + \epsilon_i \cdot \text{Softplus}((x \cdot W_{\text{noise}})_i)\]
