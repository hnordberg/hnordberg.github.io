    <text x="470" y="195" fill="#a0aec0" font-size="12" font-family="sans-serif" text-anchor="middle">\(\text{Var}(y) \approx \text{Var}(x)\) (Stable propagation)</text>

  </svg><p>Before Xavier mechanisms precisely bound network capacities, deep feedforward topologies invariably exploded or entirely collapsed mapping sequentially stochastic matrices derived haphazardly. Glorot definitively mathematically solved sequential preservation strictly forcing linear layer parameters conforming exactly to inputs mappings over bounded uniform or normal distributions.</p>

  <p>To preserve bounds mathematically across symmetric linear transformations maintaining exactly \(\text{Var}(y) = \text{Var}(x)\), the mapping matrix scale bounds inherently demand parameters explicitly compensating directly against exact specific nodes connected (fan-in and fan-out parameters). Normalizing against both forward bounding limits \(\text{Var}(W) = \frac{1}{n_{\text{in}}}\) and matching backpropagation requirements equally firmly \(\text{Var}(W) = \frac{1}{n_{\text{out}}}\), the distribution averages directly to:</p>

  \[ W_{ij} \sim \mathcal{N}\!\left(0,\; \frac{2}{n_{\text{in}} + n_{\text{out}}}\right) \quad \text{or uniform} \quad W_{ij} \sim \mathcal{U}\!\left[-\sqrt{\frac{6}{n_{\text{in}} + n_{\text{out}}}},\; \sqrt{\frac{6}{n_{\text{in}} + n_{\text{out}}}}\right] \]

  <p>For symmetric operations processing values (ex: Tanh, Sigmoid), this explicitly permanently stabilizes deep gradients bounding limits dynamically. For inherently asymmetric nodes discarding half properties structurally (ReLU zeroing maps out), Kaiming/He initialization directly supersedes it replacing constants doubling constraints tracking ReLU cut bounds strictly \( \mathcal{N}(0, \frac{2}{n_{\text{in}}}) \).</p><details>
    <summary>Derivations</summary>
    <p>Forward pass expansion evaluating sum variance bounds precisely isolates components:
    \[ \text{Var}(y_i) = \text{Var}\left(\sum_{j=1}^{n_{\text{in}}} W_{ij} x_j\right) = n_{\text{in}} \cdot \text{Var}(W_{ij}) \cdot \text{Var}(x) \]
    Setting identical propagation limits \( \text{Var}(y) = \text{Var}(x) \) identically isolates forward constraint parameters evaluating purely: \( \text{Var}(W) = \frac{1}{n_{\text{in}}} \). Matching backward requirements maps symmetric requirements directly yielding the combined average target bound \(\frac{2}{n_{\text{in}} + n_{\text{out}}}\). For uniform distributions parameterized as \(U[-a, a]\), matching standard target limits maps algebraically \( \frac{a^2}{3} = \frac{2}{n_{in} + n_{out}} \rightarrow a = \sqrt{\frac{6}{n_{in} + n_{out}}} \).</p>
