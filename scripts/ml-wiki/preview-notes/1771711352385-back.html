A parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methodology that abandons updating the model's weight matrices (like LoRA does), and instead learns a low-rank adapter to directly edit the "critical states" in the residual stream responsible for gating mathematical or logical reasoning.

Standard PEFT mechanisms like LoRA apply sparse, low-rank updates (\( \Delta W = AB \)) uniformly across weight matrices (Attn \( Q, K, V \) or MLP projections). CRFT posits that reasoning bottlenecks occur at highly specific token positions in the residual stream.

CRFT identifies these "critical" token states \( h_{\text{critical}} \) via information-flow tracing. It then applies a learned, position-specific low-rank perturbation strictly to those active hidden states:

\[ h'_{\text{critical}} = h_{\text{critical}} + BA x \]

Where \( B \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times r} \) and \( A \in \mathbb{R}^{r \times d} \) are the learned low-rank matrices (with rank \( r \ll d \)). All billions of original parameters \( \theta \) remain rigorously frozen.

Why It Matters:

By concentrating the entire parameter budget exclusively on high-leverage representation points rather than diluting edits across network weights, CRFT yields massive chain-of-thought accuracy improvements in logic benchmarks using a fraction of the parameter footprint required by standard LoRA. It aligns heavily with the Tuned Lens hypothesis that certain sequences act as discrete information routing hubs.
