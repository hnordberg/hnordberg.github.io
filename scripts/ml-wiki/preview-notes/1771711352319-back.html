A neural architecture that replaces discrete layer-by-layer computation with continuous-time dynamical systems, allowing neurons to process temporal signal histories and the network to adaptively allocate continuous computation time (depth) based on input complexity.

Standard neural networks compute representations through strict spatial sequences \( h_{l+1} = f(W h_l) \). The CTM leverages the framework of Neural ODEs to process hidden states via differential equations:

\[ \frac{dh(t)}{dt} = -\frac{h(t)}{\tau} + f(W x(t)) \]

Where \( \tau \) manages the decay rate. The primary advantage of replacing structural depth with continuous time is adaptive computation. The network natively supports halting processing at early integration limits \( t_{\text{early}} \) for trivial tasks, and churning until \( t_{\text{late}} \) for deeply complex analytical routing.
